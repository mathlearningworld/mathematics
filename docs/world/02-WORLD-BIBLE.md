# 02 — World Bible

## World identity

Math Learning World is a connected, living landscape shaped by water, craft, agriculture, observation, trade, memory, and mathematical understanding. Regions are not isolated levels. They are parts of one civilization whose systems influence one another.

## Emotional geography

The world is organized as a journey of thought:

```text
Creation → Curiosity → Discovery → Connection → Understanding → Perspective
```

Each region carries a dominant emotional purpose while supporting multiple kinds of play and learning.

## Foundational regions

### Builder's Valley — Creation

The first production anchor. A warm river valley where players gather, place, repair, and build. The region establishes agency: the world can be changed through attention and understanding.

Core experiences:

- spatial arrangement;
- quantity and resource use;
- measurement;
- structural relationships;
- cause and effect.

### Forest of Curiosity — Observation

A layered forest where growth, symmetry, cycles, branching, and hidden routes reward careful noticing.

Core experiences:

- pattern recognition;
- classification;
- sequence;
- symmetry;
- estimation.

### Crystal Mine — Discovery

A subterranean system in which evidence is incomplete and valuable structures must be inferred from clues.

Core experiences:

- grouping;
- factors and multiples;
- coordinate thinking;
- constraints;
- logical deduction.

### Home Village — Belonging

The social center of the player's journey. Knowledge becomes useful through daily life, cooperation, mentoring, trade, and improvement of shared places.

Core experiences:

- number sense;
- budgeting and exchange;
- schedules;
- data from community activity;
- contribution.

### Farm Terraces — Growth

A landscape of seasons, irrigation, area, yield, rate, and planning.

Core experiences:

- fractions and ratios;
- area;
- proportional reasoning;
- change over time;
- optimization under constraints.

### City of Mathematics — Connection

A civic and intellectual city where ideas encountered throughout the world are named, represented, compared, and connected.

Proposed districts:

- Hall of Numbers;
- Geometry Garden;
- Algebra Workshop;
- Logic Library;
- Probability Observatory;
- Function Tower;
- Equation Laboratory.

### Temple of Understanding — Insight

A place for synthesis. It does not reward memorization under pressure; it reveals whether the player can connect ideas, choose strategies, and revise reasoning.

### Final Peak — Perspective

The peak represents expanded vision rather than final completion. From above, the player can see how rivers, settlements, roads, farms, and landmarks form one system.

## World connectivity laws

1. Every major region must visibly exchange something with another region.
2. Earlier regions remain relevant after advancement.
3. New mastery should reveal deeper uses or interpretations in familiar places.
4. Travel should communicate scale and relationship, not merely load another level.
5. Landmarks must function as orientation, emotional memory, and gameplay purpose.

## Environment design template

Every region specification must define:

- emotional promise;
- visible mathematical language;
- primary player verbs;
- landmark hierarchy;
- environmental story;
- learning opportunities;
- social purpose;
- return value after mastery;
- production constraints;
- gameplay readability requirements.

## Current production anchor

Builder's Valley remains the first vertical slice. The Hero Frame and Production Environment Slice 001 should be used to validate that the World Bible can survive contact with actual runtime constraints before the world expands further.
