# 03 — Civilization Bible

## Premise

Mathematics is embedded in the life of the civilization. Citizens do not divide the world into "school mathematics" and "real life." They measure, compare, predict, design, trade, navigate, cultivate, compose, and repair as natural parts of living.

## Cultural values

- Understanding is shared, not hoarded.
- Craft is respected as applied thought.
- Asking a strong question is a form of contribution.
- Teaching another person deepens one's own mastery.
- Public systems should be understandable and fair.
- Failure during honest experimentation is normal.

## Professions as mathematical worlds

### Builders
Work with length, area, volume, angle, stability, sequence, and constraints.

### Farmers and water keepers
Work with ratios, cycles, rates, area, yield, uncertainty, and resource planning.

### Traders and stewards
Work with quantity, equivalence, budgeting, records, fairness, and forecasting.

### Navigators
Work with direction, scale, coordinates, distance, time, and optimization.

### Artisans and musicians
Work with symmetry, transformation, rhythm, proportion, pattern, and iteration.

### Researchers and observers
Work with evidence, classification, data, probability, models, and revision.

### Mentors
Help others reveal their thinking rather than simply supplying answers.

## Social learning

Learning is visible in the community through useful contribution. Players may help repair a public structure, improve a route, explain a pattern, mentor another learner, or design a more efficient system.

Mentorship must not become answer delivery. A mentor's value comes from asking, representing, demonstrating, and helping the learner regain agency.

## Economy boundary

The economy must never imply that human worth equals productivity or score. Trade and credits may model choices and constraints, but access to foundational understanding must remain fair.

## Civilization design questions

For every settlement or faction, define:

1. What do they make or maintain?
2. What patterns do they understand deeply?
3. What do they misunderstand or debate?
4. How does their environment shape their mathematics?
5. How can the player contribute without becoming the only competent person in the world?
