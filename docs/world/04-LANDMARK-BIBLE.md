# 04 — Landmark Bible

## Purpose

Landmarks are not decorative set pieces. Each one must carry orientation, emotional memory, gameplay purpose, and mathematical meaning.

## Landmark contract

Every landmark must define:

- emotional promise;
- silhouette and recognition distance;
- player approach path;
- primary interaction;
- mathematical relationship made visible;
- before-and-after state;
- return value after completion;
- day/night readability;
- gameplay safety and navigation constraints;
- production target frame.

## Initial landmark set

1. Bridge Crossing — connection through structure.
2. Forest Entrance — curiosity through partial visibility.
3. Mine Entrance — discovery through descent and evidence.
4. Home Village — belonging through shared life.
5. Farm Terrace — growth through planning and proportion.
6. Academy Terrace / City of Mathematics — connection among ideas.
7. Temple of Understanding — synthesis and insight.
8. Final Peak — perspective and interdependence.

## Hero Frame doctrine

A Hero Frame is a production target, not merely inspiration. It should establish:

- camera and composition;
- focal hierarchy;
- terrain masses;
- major architecture;
- material language;
- light direction and atmosphere;
- vegetation density;
- scale reference;
- gameplay-readable routes;
- exclusions that protect runtime clarity.

Runtime implementation should be evaluated against the frame's composition and emotional intent, not only against a checklist of included assets.

## First production authority

Production Environment Slice 001 in Builder's Valley is the first landmark validation slice:

- river corridor;
- waterfall;
- completed bridge;
- workshop terrace;
- sculpted terrain;
- vegetation;
- lighting;
- gameplay-safe integration.

No additional landmark should be promoted to production before this slice establishes a repeatable art-to-runtime pipeline.
