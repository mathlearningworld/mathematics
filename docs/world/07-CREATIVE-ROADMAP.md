# 07 — Creative Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap converts the Universe Foundation into an ordered production program. It prevents the project from expanding into many disconnected ideas before one place proves the full standard.

## Governing sequence

```text
Foundation → Target Frame → Production Slice → Runtime Validation → Learning Integration → World Expansion
```

Each stage must produce evidence before the next stage becomes authoritative.

## Phase 1 — Universe Foundation

Status: ACTIVE through PR #6.

Deliverables:

- Universe Bible;
- Learning Philosophy;
- World Bible;
- Civilization Bible;
- Landmark Bible;
- Visual Language;
- Math Learning World Design System;
- production governance and review rules.

Exit evidence:

- authority order is explicit;
- core principles do not contradict the Product Blueprint;
- Builder's Valley is confirmed as the first validation environment;
- boundaries between creative direction and runtime implementation are clear.

## Phase 2 — Builder's Valley Target Definition

Goal: turn the artistic vision into a production-ready target.

Deliverables:

- approved Hero Frame;
- scene blueprint;
- landmark contract for Bridge Crossing;
- terrain and water composition plan;
- workshop terrace plan;
- vegetation hierarchy;
- lighting target;
- gameplay readability map;
- asset and material inventory;
- measurable acceptance criteria.

Exit evidence:

- the scene can be decomposed into buildable layers;
- every major visual element has gameplay or world purpose;
- player path, camera, collision, interaction, and safe zones are known;
- the production team can estimate work without inventing the art direction during implementation.

## Phase 3 — Production Environment Slice 001

Goal: build one complete environment composition rather than many isolated assets.

Scope:

- river corridor;
- waterfall;
- completed bridge landmark;
- sculpted terrain;
- workshop terrace;
- vegetation composition;
- lighting and atmosphere;
- gameplay-safe integration.

Required production order:

1. camera and player path;
2. terrain masses and horizon;
3. river and waterfall corridor;
4. landmark silhouette;
5. workshop and secondary structures;
6. vegetation hierarchy;
7. materials and color relationships;
8. lighting and atmosphere;
9. props and micro-detail;
10. performance and gameplay validation.

Exit evidence:

- runtime captures can be compared directly against the approved Hero Frame;
- the player can navigate and interact without visual confusion;
- the environment remains readable on the target device;
- the scene communicates Creation without relying on explanatory text.

## Phase 4 — Learning Interaction Slice

Goal: prove that the world and learning philosophy work together.

Initial learning loop:

```text
Observe a structural problem
→ gather or arrange material
→ test a relationship
→ receive visible feedback
→ revise
→ complete a useful structure
→ encounter the same idea in a changed context
```

Evidence must include:

- misconception visibility;
- feedback that supports revision;
- transfer beyond the first task;
- no worksheet-style interruption;
- mastery evidence beyond one correct answer.

## Phase 5 — Builder's Valley Vertical Slice

Goal: complete one polished region loop from arrival to meaningful return.

The slice must include:

- emotional arrival;
- exploration;
- gathering;
- building or repair;
- social usefulness;
- learning evidence;
- environmental transformation;
- reason to return after completion.

Only after this phase is accepted should major world expansion begin.

## Phase 6 — Landmark Expansion

Order of concept and production planning:

1. Forest Entrance;
2. Mine Entrance;
3. Home Village;
4. Farm Terrace;
5. City of Mathematics;
6. Temple of Understanding;
7. Final Peak.

Each landmark begins with a Target Frame and contract before runtime implementation.

## Stop conditions

Pause expansion when any of the following occurs:

- new regions are being designed before Builder's Valley reaches production evidence;
- assets are added without a composition target;
- learning activities rely on question overlays rather than world interaction;
- visual detail reduces gameplay readability;
- rewards replace meaningful motivation;
- documentation grows without affecting a concrete production decision.

## Current next action

Complete the Builder's Valley Production Brief and use it as the handoff between the creative foundation and Production Environment Slice 001.
