# 00 — Universe Bible

## Status

Foundation v1 — governing creative intent.

## Core premise

Math Learning World is a universe in which understanding changes what people can perceive, build, repair, and connect. Mathematics is not presented as a school subject placed on top of the world. It is the language through which the world's patterns become visible.

The player does not arrive as a chosen hero. The player becomes capable through attention, experimentation, persistence, and understanding.

## Why this world exists

The world exists to make one truth experiential:

> The more deeply we understand relationships, patterns, quantity, space, change, and uncertainty, the more of the world becomes available to us.

Learning therefore does not merely increase a score. It changes access, interpretation, confidence, and agency.

## The laws of the universe

### 1. The world is coherent

Events have causes. Structures have relationships. Patterns can be observed. The world must reward careful attention rather than arbitrary guessing.

### 2. Understanding reveals possibility

A new idea should allow the player to notice or do something that was previously hidden: build a stronger bridge, predict a mechanism, compare routes, restore a system, or explain a pattern.

### 3. Knowledge without meaning is incomplete

Names, symbols, and formulas are useful only after they connect to experience. The universe should create the need for an idea before formally naming it whenever possible.

### 4. Mistakes are information

Failure should expose the structure of a problem. It should produce feedback, contrast, or a new clue—not shame, punishment, or meaningless repetition.

### 5. Beauty invites attention

Beauty is functional. Composition, animation, sound, and atmosphere help the player slow down, notice relationships, and care about discovery.

### 6. Creation is evidence of understanding

The strongest proof of learning is the ability to build, repair, organize, explain, predict, or help another person—not merely select the correct option.

### 7. Growth expands perspective

Progress should make the world feel larger and more connected. Higher mastery does not invalidate earlier regions; it reveals deeper layers within them.

## What is knowledge?

Knowledge is a name, fact, method, symbol, or remembered relationship.

Knowledge is valuable, but it is not the final goal.

## What is understanding?

Understanding is the ability to recognize why something works, connect it to other ideas, adapt it to a new situation, and use it with judgment.

In game terms, understanding should be visible through transfer:

- the player recognizes the same relationship in a new form;
- the player selects or invents an appropriate strategy;
- the player explains or demonstrates a reason;
- the player recovers from a mistake using evidence.

## What is mathematics in this universe?

Mathematics is the language of relationships.

It describes:

- how much;
- how many;
- how far;
- how shapes fit;
- how things change;
- how patterns continue;
- how likely events are;
- how systems remain balanced;
- how limited resources can be used wisely.

Mathematics is therefore present in rivers, buildings, trade, farming, music, navigation, machines, ecosystems, and social cooperation.

## The player's journey

The emotional journey is:

```text
Wonder → Curiosity → Experiment → Pattern → Understanding → Creation → Perspective
```

This sequence is not a rigid quest template. It is the preferred rhythm of the world.

## The central conflict

The central conflict is not good versus evil. It is clarity versus confusion, connection versus fragmentation, and agency versus helplessness.

Threats may exist, but the universe must not teach that intelligence is domination. The player's power comes from making systems more understandable, resilient, fair, and alive.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

Math Learning World must not become:

- a worksheet hidden inside fantasy art;
- a reward treadmill where points replace meaning;
- a world that punishes children for not already knowing;
- a sequence of disconnected mini-games;
- a story in which adults explain everything before the player can explore;
- a visual spectacle with no readable gameplay purpose.

## Canonical statement

> We are building a world that makes children want to understand the world—and mathematics is the language that helps them do it.
